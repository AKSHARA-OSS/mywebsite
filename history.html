Waves of Feminism

The history of feminism is often divided into four waves: 1. First Wave (19th – early 20th century): Focused on women’s right to vote (suffrage) and basic legal rights.
~ Key figures: Mary Wollstonecraft, Emmeline Pankhurst.
~ Major success: many countries granted women the right to vote.

2. Second Wave (1960s – 1980s): Focused on equality in workplace, education, and family life.
~ Slogan: “The personal is political.”
~ Fought against gender roles and for reproductive rights.

3. Third Wave (1990s – 2000s): Focused on individual identity, diversity, and inclusion.
~ Emphasized that women’s experiences differ by race, class, and culture.

4. Fourth Wave (2010s – present): Driven by the internet and social media (like the #MeToo movement).
~ Focuses on sexual harassment, body positivity, and intersectional equality.
